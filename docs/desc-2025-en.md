`The 34th edition of the symposium "Pedogenetic Factors and Processes in the Temperate Zone", organised by the Faculty of Geography and Geology at "Alexandru Ioan Cuza" University of Iași and the Centre for Geographical Research of the Romanian Academy – Iași Branch, together with the Research and Development Institute for Hunting and Mountain Resources in Miercurea Ciuc and the Harghita Office of Pedological and Agrochemical Studies, was held between 1–5 October 2025 in Miercurea Ciuc.`

`The field trip focused on two key morpho-structural units of Harghita County — the Ciuc Depression and the Harghita Mountains — with the primary aim of analysing the soil assemblage as a fundamental resource of the mountain landscape. The investigation targeted units shaped by intense Neogene volcanism spanning the Sarmatian to Pleistocene, which gave rise to an imposing mountain chain and a morphotectonic trough with a prolonged lacustrine evolution.`

`The Ciuc Depression is a tectonic and volcanic barrier unit divided into three distinct compartments — northern (Mădăraș), central (Miercurea Ciuc) and lower (Tușnad) — separated by the eruptive and crystalline thresholds at Racu and Jigodin. The depression's relief is dominated by vast alluvial plains and a well-defined terrace system, especially the 20 m relative-altitude level. Excess moisture and hygrophilous vegetation have favoured the development of Gleysols and Histosols. Climatically, the region is known as Romania's "cold pole".`

`The Harghita Mountains display two main geomorphological levels: the volcanic cone level (central-eastern), comprising ten major volcanic structures reaching 1,800 m at Harghita-Mădăraș Peak, and the volcano-sedimentary plateau level (western and south-western), formed by successions of pyroclastic and epiclastic deposits. The predominantly kaolinitic clays at the top of the volcano-sedimentary complex, produced by in situ weathering of fine volcanics, have driven an extremely advanced pedogenetic evolution characterised by the dominance of Albic Stagnic Luvisols and Stagnosols.`

`The first day's itinerary (3 October) covered the lower Ciuc Depression: Miercurea Ciuc – Sâncrăieni – Sânsimion – Vrabia – Tușnad – Băile Tușnad, studying four soil profiles (Cambic Phaeozem, Teric Histosol, two Eutric Psamosols). The second day (4 October) crossed the Harghita Mountains from east to west: Miercurea Ciuc – Harghita Băi – Vlăhița – Băile Homorod – Zetea – Izvorul Mureșului – Miercurea Ciuc, studying a Dystric Skeletic Andosol and an Albic Planosolic Stagnic Luvisol.`
